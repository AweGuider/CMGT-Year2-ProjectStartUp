Sadly, the dreamer's words prove as false as they always do. Nothing is out of the ordinary as he enters the stuffed but homey kitchen. 

Jars and pots crowd the counters these days, some already filled with preserved food, others still waiting to be filled with pickled vegetables or fruit turned into sweet jam.

His mother stands by the stove, stirring in a pot. She is wearing a tattered apron that is covered in colorful splatters. They are red and blue and pink like the jams she cooked during the last days. The pattern the stains form on the faric looks as if a child had painted it with its fingers.
